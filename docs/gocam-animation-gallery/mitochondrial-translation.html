Mitochondrial gene expression in Schizosaccharomyces pombe

A single pathway figure rendered faithfully from the GO-CAM causal model. The mitochondrial central dogma runs top‑to‑bottom: an mtDNA locus → multicistronic primary transcript → mature mRNA → nascent polypeptide → respiratory‑chain complex. Every has_input and has_output of each molecular activity is drawn as a coloured, shaped token, so the reader can see at a glance which molecular form of a gene each regulator acts on.

Edge colour encodes the molecular form of the entity, which is licensed by the activity's GO molecular function rather than stored on the entity itself: a translation‑activator, mRNA‑binding or exoribonuclease activity acts on the mRNA; a peptidyl‑transferase, release‑factor or complex‑assembly activity acts on the protein; a DNA‑binding transcription factor would act on the gene/DNA (no such activity occurs in this model). The same locus (e.g. cox1) therefore appears both as an mRNA input to translational activators and as a protein output of the ribosome.
